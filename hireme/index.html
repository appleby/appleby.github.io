Hire Appleby

May 1, 2019

If you're reading this, you probably received from me a resume or an email inquiring about a job. The purpose of this page is to provide a bit more context about me than will fit on a resume, in order to help you decided whether or not you want to interview me. You can find a copy of my resume here. My github profile is github.com/appleby.

Who am I?

I am a software engineer with approximately 5 years of professional experience in the network security sector. But I am not a security expert, just a backend/systems software generalist. At my previous company, I worked on a network scanning appliance that ran on FreeBSD and Linux that did host, port, OS, application, and vulnerability detection. I have professional experience programming in C/C++ and Python.

Where am I?

I currently live in Mexico. Ideally, I'd like to find a company that lets me work remotely, but I am a U.S. citizen and am willing to relocate for the right job.

What's up with the 5-year resume gap?

The company I worked at previously was acquired in 2013. I stuck around for ~8 months after the acquisition, then resigned. I left on good terms. During the period December 2013 - May 2019, I was not looking for work.

Why should you hire me?

You should hire me if you are looking for a decent mid-level engineer. You should not hire me if you need a rockstar/ninja/10x engineer.

How should you hire me?

My preference is for companies that are willing to try a short-term contract-to-hire process. You pay me a contracting rate for $n$ weeks, where $n$ is negotiable, but ideally $1 < n < 7$. At the end of the contracting period, you can offer me a full-time job or not. If so, I can accept or decline. My goal is to trick some outstanding company into making me an offer, so this is my preferred hiring method. I believe this method is also better for the hiring company, but that's up to you. Of course, if you are unwilling or unable to try contract-to-hire, I'm happy to go through your standard hiring process.
